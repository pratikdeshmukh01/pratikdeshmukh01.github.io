About

I am deeply interested in understanding how businesses create value, how capital is allocated, and how financial markets reflect these dynamics over time.

My focus lies at the intersection of investing, business analysis, and financial decision-making. I enjoy studying companies, analyzing financial statements, evaluating business models, and exploring the factors that drive long-term growth and shareholder value.

Alongside my academic foundation in commerce and finance, I continue to develop practical skills in financial modeling, valuation, business analytics, and market research. I believe that sound investment decisions emerge from a combination of rigorous analysis, intellectual curiosity, and a long-term perspective.

Travel

Geography shapes perspective. From the extreme altitudes of the Himalayas to the structural density of India's commercial hubs, every expedition acts as a lens into human and economic behavior.

Badrinath Dham

At 3,133m in the Garhwal Himalayas — where devotion meets the raw edge of the world. One of the Char Dham, and one of the most transformative journeys of my life.

30.7433° N, 79.4938° E
🌿

Mana Village

The last inhabited village before the Indo-Tibet border. A place that feels like the edge of the map — where India ends and the mountains take over.

30.7798° N, 79.5631° E
🕉️

Ujjain

One of the seven sacred cities of India. Home of the Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga — a city where time feels different, older, slower, more permanent.

23.1765° N, 75.7885° E
🌄

Rishikesh

The Yoga Capital of the World, where the Ganga descends from the mountains into the plains. A city of seekers and sadhus, bridges and bells.

30.0869° N, 78.2676° E
🪔

Kashi (Varanasi)

The oldest living city in the world. Every evening the Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat is a reminder that some traditions outlast everything — empires, ideologies, time itself.

25.3176° N, 82.9739° E
🏹

Ayodhya

The birthplace of Shri Ram — a city that carries the weight of civilisation in its very soil. To walk here is to walk through thousands of years of cultural memory.

26.7922° N, 82.1998° E
🐚

Rameshwaram

Where the land meets the sea and the legend of Ram Setu begins. The Ramanathaswamy Temple corridors are the longest in the world — a kilometre of pillars and prayer.

9.2876° N, 79.3129° E
🛕

Madurai

The city that never sleeps — the Meenakshi Amman Temple towers over everything, its gopurams covered in thousands of sculpted figures, a city within a city.

9.9252° N, 78.1198° E
🌊

Puri

Home of the Jagannath Temple and the great Rath Yatra. Standing before the sea here is different — the Bay of Bengal carries a particular vastness that stills the mind.

19.8135° N, 85.8312° E
